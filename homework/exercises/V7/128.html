Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 24 \\ -2 \\ 9 \\ 18 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -21 \\ 0 \\ -8 \\ -19 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
