Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 3 \\ -12 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 21 \\ -13 \\ 28 \\ 14 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -61 \\ 33 \\ -78 \\ -34 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -22 \\ 6 \\ -24 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
