Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 17 \\ -11 \\ 17 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -12 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ -58 \\ 34 \\ -44 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
