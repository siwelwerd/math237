Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ -13 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ -9 \\ 10 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
