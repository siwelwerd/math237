Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
