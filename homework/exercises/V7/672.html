Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ -12 \\ 3 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -10 \\ 9 \\ 1 \\ -9 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
