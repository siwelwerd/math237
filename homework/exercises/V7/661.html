Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -28 \\ 1 \\ 10 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -13 \\ 64 \\ -13 \\ -25 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ -80 \\ 20 \\ 32 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ -24 \\ 13 \\ 11 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
