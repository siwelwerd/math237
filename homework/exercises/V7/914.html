Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ -8 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 5 \\ -9 \\ -11 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -9 \\ 13 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
