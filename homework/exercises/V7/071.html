Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -7 \\ 12 \\ -11 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 26 \\ -32 \\ 29 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -18 \\ 34 \\ -36 \\ -20 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
