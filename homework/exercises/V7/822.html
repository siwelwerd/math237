Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -2 \\ 0 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 9 \\ -9 \\ 0 \\ 36 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
