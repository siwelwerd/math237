Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ -10 \\ -9 \\ -8 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ -8 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ -18 \\ -14 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 8 \\ 7 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
