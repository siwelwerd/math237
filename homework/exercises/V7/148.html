Find a basis for the subspace

\[W=\mathrm{span}\ \left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ 16 \\ 21 \\ 17 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -22 \\ -24 \\ -44 \\ -28 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\right\}.\]

Be sure to explain why your result is a basis.

Answer.

A basis of \(W\) is \(\left\{\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\right\}\).
