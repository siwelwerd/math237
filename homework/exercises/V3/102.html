V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 102)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 8 \\ -4 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ -8 \\ 19 \\ -20 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 8 \\ -4 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ -8 \\ 19 \\ -20 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) do span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
