V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 610)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -14 \\ 1 \\ -9 \\ 19 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -14 \\ 1 \\ -9 \\ 19 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
