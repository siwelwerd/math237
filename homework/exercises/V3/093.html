V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 93)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -16 \\ 14 \\ 2 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 36 \\ -34 \\ -2 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -16 \\ 14 \\ 2 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 36 \\ -34 \\ -2 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
