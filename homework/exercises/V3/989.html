V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 989)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) do span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
