V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 680)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 10 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ -4 \\ 11 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 10 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ -4 \\ 11 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
