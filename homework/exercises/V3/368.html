V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 368)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 14 \\ -24 \\ 0 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 26 \\ -40 \\ 8 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 14 \\ -24 \\ 0 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 26 \\ -40 \\ 8 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
