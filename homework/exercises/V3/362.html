V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 362)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -7 \\ 2 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 28 \\ -2 \\ -39 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -7 \\ 2 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 28 \\ -2 \\ -39 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
