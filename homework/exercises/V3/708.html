V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 708)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 9 \\ -3 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 9 \\ -3 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
