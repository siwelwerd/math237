V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 147)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ 24 \\ 10 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -45 \\ -61 \\ -20 \\ -23 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ 24 \\ 10 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -45 \\ -61 \\ -20 \\ -23 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
