V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 55)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) do span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
