V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 601)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ 7 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 16 \\ 10 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ 7 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 16 \\ 10 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
