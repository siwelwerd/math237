V3 - Spanning sets (ver. 568)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ -21 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -4 \\ 6 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ -21 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -4 \\ 6 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) do not span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
