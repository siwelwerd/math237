V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 568)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -6 \\ -7 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -21 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -12 \\ -4 \\ 12 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -6 \\ -7 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -21 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -12 \\ -4 \\ 12 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\).
