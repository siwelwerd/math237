V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 661)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -12 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -19 \\ 30 \\ -19 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -17 \\ 39 \\ -17 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -12 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -19 \\ 30 \\ -19 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -17 \\ 39 \\ -17 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\).
