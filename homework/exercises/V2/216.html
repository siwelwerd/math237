V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 216)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -13 \\ -4 \\ 11 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -13 \\ -4 \\ 11 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\).
