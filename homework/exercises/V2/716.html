V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 716)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 8 \\ -1 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 11 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 21 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 8 \\ -1 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 11 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 21 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\).
