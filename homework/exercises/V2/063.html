V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 63)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ 9 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -17 \\ -12 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ 9 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -17 \\ -12 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\).
