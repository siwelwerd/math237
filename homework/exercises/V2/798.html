V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 798)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -7 \\ -9 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 12 \\ 6 \\ -12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 26 \\ 12 \\ -25 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 18 \\ 12 \\ -21 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -7 \\ -9 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 12 \\ 6 \\ -12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 26 \\ 12 \\ -25 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 18 \\ 12 \\ -21 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).
