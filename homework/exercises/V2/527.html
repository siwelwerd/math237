V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 527)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).
