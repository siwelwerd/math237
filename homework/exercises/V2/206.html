V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 206)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 8 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ -9 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 8 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ -9 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\).
