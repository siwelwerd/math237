V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 596)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -9 \\ -6 \\ 7 \\ 9 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -9 \\ -6 \\ 7 \\ 9 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\).
