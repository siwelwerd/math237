V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 83)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ 8 \\ 9 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ 8 \\ 9 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).
