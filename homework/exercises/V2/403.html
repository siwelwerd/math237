V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 403)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 10 \\ 0 \\ -8 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 7 \\ -22 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 10 \\ 0 \\ -8 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 7 \\ -22 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).
