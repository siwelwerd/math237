V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 602)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 10 \\ 0 \\ -18 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -15 \\ 0 \\ 27 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 4 \\ -17 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 10 \\ 0 \\ -18 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -15 \\ 0 \\ 27 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 4 \\ -17 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\).
