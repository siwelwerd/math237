V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 376)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 16 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 32 \\ -8 \\ 8 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 28 \\ -7 \\ 12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 16 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 32 \\ -8 \\ 8 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 28 \\ -7 \\ 12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).
