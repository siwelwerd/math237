V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 717)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ -2 \\ 8 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ 6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 6 \\ -12 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ -2 \\ 8 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ 6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 6 \\ -12 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\).
