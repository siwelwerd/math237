V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 991)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 9 \\ 13 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 13 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 9 \\ 13 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 13 \end{array}\right]\).
