V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 880)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).
