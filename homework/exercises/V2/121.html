V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 121)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -9 \\ -6 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -9 \\ -6 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\).
