V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 144)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 3 \\ -10 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 6 \\ 7 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -13 \\ 14 \\ 16 \\ 10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 17 \\ -10 \\ -14 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 6 \\ 3 \\ -10 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 6 \\ 7 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -13 \\ 14 \\ 16 \\ 10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 17 \\ -10 \\ -14 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).
