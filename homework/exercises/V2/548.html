V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 548)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ -12 \\ 18 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ -12 \\ 18 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\).
