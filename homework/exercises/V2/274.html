V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 274)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ -6 \\ 9 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -30 \\ 21 \\ -26 \\ -22 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 9 \\ -6 \\ 9 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -30 \\ 21 \\ -26 \\ -22 \end{array}\right]\).
