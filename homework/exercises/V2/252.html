V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 252)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -10 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -10 \\ 8 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -11 \\ -11 \\ 10 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -10 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -10 \\ 8 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -11 \\ -11 \\ 10 \\ 8 \end{array}\right]\).
