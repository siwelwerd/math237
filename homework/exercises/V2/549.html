V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 549)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -6 \\ 8 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -7 \\ 21 \\ -21 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 12 \\ -14 \\ 8 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -6 \\ 8 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -7 \\ 21 \\ -21 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 12 \\ -14 \\ 8 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).
