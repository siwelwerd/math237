V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 961)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -2 \\ -7 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ -14 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -2 \\ -7 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ -14 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\).
