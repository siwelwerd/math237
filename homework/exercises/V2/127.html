V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 127)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ -8 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 14 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ -7 \\ 19 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ -2 \\ -8 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 14 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ -7 \\ 19 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).
