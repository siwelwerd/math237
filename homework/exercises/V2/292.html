V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 292)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -7 \\ 7 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 32 \\ -33 \\ -13 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -10 \\ 2 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -7 \\ 7 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 13 \\ 32 \\ -33 \\ -13 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -10 \\ -10 \\ 2 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\).
