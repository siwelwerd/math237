V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 947)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -10 \\ -3 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 7 \\ -18 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -10 \\ -3 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 7 \\ -18 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\).
