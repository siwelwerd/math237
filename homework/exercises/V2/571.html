V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 571)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 8 \\ -7 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 13 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 8 \\ -7 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ -3 \\ 13 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).
