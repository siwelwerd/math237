V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 466)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -5 \\ -9 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 7 \\ 6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 15 \\ 14 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 19 \\ -14 \\ -23 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ -5 \\ -9 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 7 \\ 6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 15 \\ 14 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 19 \\ -14 \\ -23 \\ -1 \end{array}\right]\).
