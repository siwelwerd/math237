V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 29)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 2 \\ -16 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 8 \\ 17 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -1 \\ 6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -6 \\ 2 \\ -16 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 8 \\ 17 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\).
