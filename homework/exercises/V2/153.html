V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 153)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -7 \\ -2 \\ -6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} -7 \\ -2 \\ -6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) is a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\).
