Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 14 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -16 \\ 6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 14 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -16 \\ 6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
