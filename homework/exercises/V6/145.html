Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \\ 8 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 16 \\ -6 \\ -25 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \\ 8 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 16 \\ -6 \\ -25 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
