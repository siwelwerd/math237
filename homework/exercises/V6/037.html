Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -16 \\ -10 \\ 3 \\ 6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 47 \\ 17 \\ 3 \\ -12 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -16 \\ -10 \\ 3 \\ 6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 47 \\ 17 \\ 3 \\ -12 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
