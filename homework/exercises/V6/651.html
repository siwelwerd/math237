Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 0 \\ 6 \\ -9 \\ 9 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 4 \\ -26 \\ 32 \\ -34 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 28 \\ 6 \\ -43 \\ 54 \\ -57 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 0 \\ 6 \\ -9 \\ 9 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 4 \\ -26 \\ 32 \\ -34 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 28 \\ 6 \\ -43 \\ 54 \\ -57 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
