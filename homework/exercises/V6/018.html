Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 12 \\ 12 \\ -9 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 12 \\ 12 \\ -9 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
