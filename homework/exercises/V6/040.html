Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 11 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 11 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
