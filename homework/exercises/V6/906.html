Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
