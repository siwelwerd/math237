Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 6 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 18 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 6 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 18 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
