Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 20 \\ 16 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -7 \\ 8 \\ 13 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 20 \\ 16 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -7 \\ 8 \\ 13 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
