Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -4 \\ -12 \\ -16 \\ -14 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 33 \\ 16 \\ 43 \\ 50 \\ 43 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -4 \\ -12 \\ -16 \\ -14 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 33 \\ 16 \\ 43 \\ 50 \\ 43 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
