Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 10 \\ 13 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -20 \\ 23 \\ 33 \\ -2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 10 \\ 13 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -20 \\ 23 \\ 33 \\ -2 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
