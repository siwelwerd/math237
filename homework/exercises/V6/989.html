Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -11 \\ 13 \\ -4 \\ -11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -11 \\ 13 \\ -4 \\ -11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
