Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 8 \\ 12 \\ 0 \\ 14 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ -12 \\ -28 \\ -4 \\ -27 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -32 \\ 20 \\ 40 \\ 4 \\ 41 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 8 \\ 12 \\ 0 \\ 14 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ -12 \\ -28 \\ -4 \\ -27 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -32 \\ 20 \\ 40 \\ 4 \\ 41 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
