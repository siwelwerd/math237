Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 21 \\ -10 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ -24 \\ 15 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -5 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 21 \\ -10 \\ 11 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ -24 \\ 15 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
