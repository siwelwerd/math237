Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^3\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 0 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^3\).
