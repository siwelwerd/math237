Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -12 \\ -8 \\ 9 \\ 20 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 12 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -20 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -12 \\ -8 \\ 9 \\ 20 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 12 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -20 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
