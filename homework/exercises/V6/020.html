Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ -12 \\ -11 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ -12 \\ -11 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
