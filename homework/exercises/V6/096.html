Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 12 \\ -2 \\ 10 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 12 \\ -2 \\ 10 \\ 7 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
