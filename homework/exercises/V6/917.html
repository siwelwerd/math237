Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 23 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 22 \\ 2 \\ -10 \\ 10 \\ -64 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 23 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 22 \\ 2 \\ -10 \\ 10 \\ -64 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^5\).
