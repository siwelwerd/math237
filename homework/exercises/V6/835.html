Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are or are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^3\)

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are not a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^3\).
