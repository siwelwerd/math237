V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 164)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 24 \\ -7 \\ -20 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 24 \\ -7 \\ -20 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
