V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 993)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 8 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -12 \\ -8 \\ 8 \\ -18 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 8 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -12 \\ -8 \\ 8 \\ -18 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
