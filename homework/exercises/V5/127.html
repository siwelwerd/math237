V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 127)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 6 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -7 \\ -11 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 6 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -7 \\ -11 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
