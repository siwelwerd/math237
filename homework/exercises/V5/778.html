V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 778)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly independent .
