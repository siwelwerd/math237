V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 25)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -9 \\ -7 \\ 1 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 8 \\ 18 \\ 3 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -9 \\ -7 \\ 1 \\ -10 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 12 \\ 8 \\ 18 \\ 3 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
