V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 309)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 20 \\ -4 \\ -20 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 5 \\ -10 \\ -5 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 20 \\ -4 \\ -20 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 5 \\ -10 \\ -5 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
