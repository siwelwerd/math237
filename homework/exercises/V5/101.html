V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 101)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -9 \\ -15 \\ -15 \\ -9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -21 \\ -4 \\ -26 \\ -14 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ -9 \\ -15 \\ -15 \\ -9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -21 \\ -4 \\ -26 \\ -14 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
