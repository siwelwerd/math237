V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 185)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -7 \\ 15 \\ 14 \\ 1 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -7 \\ 15 \\ 14 \\ 1 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
