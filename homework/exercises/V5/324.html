V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 324)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -15 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -7 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -12 \\ -10 \\ -2 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -15 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -7 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -12 \\ -10 \\ -2 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
