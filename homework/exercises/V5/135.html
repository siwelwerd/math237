V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 135)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 9 \\ -6 \\ -9 \\ -26 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 9 \\ -6 \\ -9 \\ -26 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
