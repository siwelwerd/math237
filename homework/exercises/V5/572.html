V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 572)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 9 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 9 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
