V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 858)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ -2 \\ 6 \\ -10 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ -2 \\ 6 \\ -10 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
