V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 704)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 7 \\ -15 \\ 8 \\ 14 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 7 \\ -15 \\ 8 \\ 14 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
