V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 211)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 12 \\ 2 \\ -7 \\ -19 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 12 \\ 2 \\ -7 \\ -19 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
