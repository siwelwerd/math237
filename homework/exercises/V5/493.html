V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 493)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 14 \\ 23 \\ -8 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -13 \\ -16 \\ -4 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 14 \\ 23 \\ -8 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -13 \\ -16 \\ -4 \\ -9 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
