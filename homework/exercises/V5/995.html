V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 995)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ 14 \\ 10 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 20 \\ 14 \\ 10 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
