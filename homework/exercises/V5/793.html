V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 793)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 27 \\ 1 \\ 9 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -5 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 27 \\ 1 \\ 9 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
