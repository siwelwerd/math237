V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 733)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 6 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 6 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
