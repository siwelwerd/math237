V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 254)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 8 \\ -15 \\ 9 \\ 24 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ -2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 8 \\ -15 \\ 9 \\ 24 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
