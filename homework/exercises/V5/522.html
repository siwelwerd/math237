V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 522)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 6 \\ 9 \\ 12 \\ -9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -16 \\ -4 \\ -16 \\ -14 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 6 \\ 9 \\ 12 \\ -9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -16 \\ -4 \\ -16 \\ -14 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
