V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 176)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -18 \\ 6 \\ -3 \\ 12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -18 \\ 6 \\ -3 \\ 12 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
