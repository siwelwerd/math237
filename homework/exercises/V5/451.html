V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 451)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly independent .
