V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 543)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 10 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 8 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 10 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 8 \\ 6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
