V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 992)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 5 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -13 \\ 4 \\ 22 \\ -12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 5 \\ -2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -13 \\ 4 \\ 22 \\ -12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
