V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 464)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -6 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 10 \\ 27 \\ 13 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -6 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 10 \\ 27 \\ 13 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
