V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 570)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -5 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ 19 \\ 10 \\ -22 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -5 \\ 3 \\ 5 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -17 \\ 19 \\ 10 \\ -22 \\ 15 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 4 \\ -5 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
