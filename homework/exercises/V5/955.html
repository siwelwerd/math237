V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 955)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ -3 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ 14 \\ 13 \\ 20 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ -3 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 2 \\ 14 \\ 13 \\ 20 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
