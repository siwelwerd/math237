V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 880)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 4 \\ -8 \\ 15 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -5 \\ -2 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 4 \\ -8 \\ 15 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
