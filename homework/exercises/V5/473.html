V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 473)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ 6 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -23 \\ -15 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 0 \\ -5 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ 6 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -23 \\ -15 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
