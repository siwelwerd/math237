V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 926)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ -3 \\ 15 \\ -21 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 0 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ -3 \\ 15 \\ -21 \\ 15 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
