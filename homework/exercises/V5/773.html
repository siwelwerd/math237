V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 773)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 4 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly independent .
