V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 931)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 6 \\ 23 \\ -20 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -10 \\ 2 \\ 15 \\ -7 \\ -12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 6 \\ 23 \\ -20 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -10 \\ 2 \\ 15 \\ -7 \\ -12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
