V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 223)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 37 \\ 19 \\ 2 \\ 27 \\ 22 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -5 \\ 5 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 37 \\ 19 \\ 2 \\ 27 \\ 22 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
