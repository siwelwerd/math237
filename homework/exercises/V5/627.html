V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 627)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 1 \\ -7 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -14 \\ 11 \\ 7 \\ -8 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ -3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 1 \\ -7 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -14 \\ 11 \\ 7 \\ -8 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
