V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 241)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -5 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ 10 \\ 6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 16 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -5 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ 10 \\ 6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 16 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -13 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
