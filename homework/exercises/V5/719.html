V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 719)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ -8 \\ 1 \\ -12 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 1 \\ 13 \\ -10 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -2 \\ -8 \\ 1 \\ -12 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 16 \\ 1 \\ 13 \\ -10 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
