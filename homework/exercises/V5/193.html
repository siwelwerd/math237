V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 193)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -13 \\ 0 \\ -9 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -20 \\ -2 \\ -17 \\ -16 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -13 \\ 0 \\ -9 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -20 \\ -2 \\ -17 \\ -16 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
