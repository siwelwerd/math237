V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 436)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -6 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 6 \\ 11 \\ 15 \\ 14 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -6 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ 6 \\ 11 \\ 15 \\ 14 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \\ -6 \\ -4 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
