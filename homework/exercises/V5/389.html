V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 389)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 8 \\ 8 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -6 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 8 \\ 8 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
