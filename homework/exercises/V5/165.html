V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 165)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 13 \\ 9 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 5 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -11 \\ 13 \\ 9 \\ -6 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
