V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 853)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -10 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 7 \\ -13 \\ -17 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -1 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -10 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ -7 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 7 \\ -13 \\ -17 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
