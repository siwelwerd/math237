V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 409)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 10 \\ 2 \\ 9 \\ 9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -12 \\ 10 \\ 2 \\ 9 \\ 9 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -2 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
