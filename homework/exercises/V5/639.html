V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 639)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -10 \\ -7 \\ -17 \\ 13 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ -21 \\ -8 \\ 9 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 5 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -10 \\ -7 \\ -17 \\ 13 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ -21 \\ -8 \\ 9 \\ 12 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
