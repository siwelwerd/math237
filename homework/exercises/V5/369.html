V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 369)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -6 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ -3 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -6 \\ -3 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly independent .
