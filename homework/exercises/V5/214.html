V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 214)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ 18 \\ 15 \\ 12 \\ 18 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -21 \\ -25 \\ -13 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ -6 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ 3 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 15 \\ 18 \\ 15 \\ 12 \\ 18 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ -3 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -21 \\ -25 \\ -13 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
