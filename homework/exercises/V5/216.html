V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 216)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 16 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ -6 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 16 \\ 12 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
