V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 970)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -8 \\ -16 \\ -20 \\ -6 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ -6 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ 5 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ -1 \\ -6 \\ -6 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -6 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -8 \\ -16 \\ -20 \\ -6 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
