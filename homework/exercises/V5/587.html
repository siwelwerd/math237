V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 587)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 2 \\ -13 \\ -9 \\ -12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -7 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 2 \\ -5 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ 5 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 2 \\ -13 \\ -9 \\ -12 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -7 \\ 8 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 4 \\ -2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
