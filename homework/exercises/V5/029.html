V5 - Linear Independence (ver. 29)

Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 18 \\ 21 \\ -21 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent or linearly independent.

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -6 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 1 \\ -5 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ -5 \\ 2 \\ 4 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 18 \\ 21 \\ -21 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]\) are linearly dependent .
